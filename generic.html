New York City's Income to Rent Problem

New York, NY -— At this point it’s a cliché, the rents are too damn high, but it rings true in almost every major city in the USA. The US bureau of Labor Statistics rents had risen 4.8% since 2021, and for many that can be the unmountable first hurdle towards homeownership. While the issue reaches all corners of the nation, New York City consistently rank amongst the top cities with majority renters, and is notoriously expensive to live in (the cities colloquial saying ‘you can’t leave your house for less than $20’), so we focused our research to the five boroughs of NYC. That and the data sets for the nation would have sent me to an early grave.

We used rental data from Street Easy’s Data Dashboard to extrapolate the average rent of the five boroughs from 2011 to 2020, and compared the percentage increase to that of the US Census Bureau’s Data Sets for the average income across the five boroughs.

The average rent was aggregated and averaged for each year, from 2011 to 2020, a time span limitation due to Census record release, to the show the percent increase of average income over those years within the five boroughs.
